1. Continuing application:
Upon submitting application, transfer the current deposit to the new application. In your current application page, you can find the Deposit Transfer/Refund Request button at the bottom. Fill out the request and provide the new application number from your new term application. The carried forward deposit will complete your application. NOTE: Your application without deposit transfer/payment will not be processed.

2. New application:
Upon submitting application, make a deposit of 200,000 KRW to:

Make sure to write your name and Student ID as the remittance information. After transferring the money, fax wire transfer confirmation stub to the residence life office at 82-2-926-3464, accompanied by a copy of your online residence hall application.
Note that the sender is responsible for any transfer charges, including intermediary and beneficiary bank fees. If the actual amount received is less than 200,000 KRW, the balance in KRW should be additionally deposited after the student's arrival.
Helpful tips: Convert your currency into KRW and assume any transfer charges before wiring.

Any incomplete application will not be processed. For example, if the deposit amount is not paid by due date, your application will not be processed.
After your application, you'll receive a letter containing your room assignment and the total amount of residence hall fee to be deposited. If you do not hear from us by the expected confirmation date, please contact Residence Life immediately. For the room assignment date, refer to the Important Dates above.

2. Payment methods

In Person
Find a Hana Bank branch that is closest to your work or home. Bring your billing statement to your branch of choice and pay by cash during the payment period. Please note that in person payment is available between 9:30 am and 4:30 pm on weekdays, except holidays.

Online
Pay by Internet Banking - Electronic Funds Transfer only from a Korean bank account, during the payment period. Please note that online payment is available between 9:30 am and 4:30 pm on weekdays, except holidays.
Note that the sender is responsible for any transfer charges, including intermediary and beneficiary bank fees. If the actual amount received is less than 200,000 KRW, the balance in KRW should be additionally deposited after the student's arrival.
Helpful tips: Convert your currency into KRW and assume any transfer charges before wiring.

Late fee will apply after the due date. Failure to make the payment by due date may result in forfeiture of your deposit and immediate termination of your residence hall contract.
For International Students for Fall 2006 Session only:
If you are an international student of Korea University exchange programs, then you may defer your residence hall fee. Students participating in the deferred payment plan must download the payment deferral application/agreement and mail it to Residence Life(Anam Residence Life - International Residence, Korea University, Anam-dong Seongbuk-gu, Seoul 02841) by August 9, 2006. The residence hall fee should be paid between September 6 and September 8, 2006. Failure to pay the amount due by the dates specified in the Deferred Payment Agreement, shall result in an additional 30,000 KRW late fee and loss of the fee deferral privilege for future sessions.
Note that the sender is responsible for any transfer charges, including intermediary and beneficiary bank fees. The best method to make payments is to open a Korean bank account and deposit money into it upon your arrival. Then you will be able to easily wire money into the Korea University International Student Residence Hana Bank account.

If the amount is not paid by the due date, your room assignment will be cancelled and your deposit will be forfeited.

If you decide to cancel during any stage of this process you must notify the Office of Residence Life as soon as possible to reduce the risk of penalty. Please see the contract for more information.
